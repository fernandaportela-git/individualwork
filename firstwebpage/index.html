-------- Easy and Fast Dessert Recipes --------

3-Ingredient Nutella Brownies

Ingredients:

  • 1 ¼ cups Nutella (or any hazelnut cocoa spread)
  • 2 Large eggs
  • ½ cup All-purpose flour
  • Optional: A pinch of sea salt for the top

Instructions:

Preheat your oven to 180°C and grease a small square baking pan. In a medium bowl, whisk the eggs and Nutella together until the mixture is smooth and well combined, then gently fold in the flour until no white streaks remain. Pour the thick batter into your prepared pan, smoothing the top with a spatula, and bake for 20 to 25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with just a few moist crumbs. Let the brownies cool completely in the pan before slicing to ensure they set into that perfect fudgy texture.

2-Ingredient Pineapple Cake

Ingredients:

  • 1 box (approx. 430g) Angel Food cake mix (or Vanilla cake mix)
  • 1 can (560g) Crushed pineapple in juice (do not drain)

Instructions:

Preheat your oven to 175°C and prepare a 9x13 inch baking dish (though you don't even need to grease it if using Angel Food mix). In a large bowl, dump the dry cake mix and the entire can of crushed pineapple, including all the juice from the can. Stir the two ingredients together by hand until the batter is fluffy and fully combined, then pour it into the baking dish. Bake for 25 to 30 minutes until the top is golden brown and the cake springs back when touched, creating a light, tropical dessert that tastes great on its own or with a dollop of whipped cream.

Easy Peanut Butter Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Creamy peanut butter
  • 1 cup White sugar
  • 1 Large egg

Instructions:

Preheat your oven to 180°C and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a bowl, mix the peanut butter, sugar, and egg together until the dough becomes stiff and easy to handle. Roll the dough into small balls, about one inch in diameter, and place them on the baking sheet spaced two inches apart. Use a fork to press a golden-brown "X" pattern into the top of each ball, flattening them slightly, and bake for 8 to 10 minutes until the edges are just barely set. Be sure to let them cool on the baking sheet for at least 5 minutes before moving them, as they are very soft when they first come out of the oven.
